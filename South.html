Domingo's Cat

The legend of Domingo’s Cat originated in Brazil and was told orally.
Because of this, it is difficult to pinpoint when exactly the legend of
Domingo’s Cat began, though it has much in common with The Master Cat or
Puss in Boots from the 17th century. One of the earliest publications of
the story was in Elsie Spicer Eells’ Tales of Giants From Brazil, published in 1918.
It’s a short fable aimed at children to teach them the value of loyalty and kindness.

The fable is about Domingo, a poor man forced to sell his belongings for food.
His only possession left is his cat. Domingo promises not to sell the cat,
and the cat in turn promises to help him get money for food. The cat then proceeds
to help Domingo get money, a wife who is the daughter of the king, and a beautiful
palace of silver, gold, and diamonds. The cat then disappears to help others in need.
Domingo's Cat remains a popular story in South America, and across the world, today.
